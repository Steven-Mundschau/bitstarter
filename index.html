Recently we have all been made aware of the ability and willingness of the government to collect and store the personal communications of every citizen. The major phone companies have also displayed their willingness to let the government listen in and read everything we say. This project will propose and implement an open protocol for peer-to-peer encryption of text data. This is intended to work over existing text infrastuctures such as text messaging, e-mail, Twitter, Facebook, etc.

By using existing communication channels, this product will not introduce a dependency upon our servers. Once you have an application using our encryption system installed, your messages can be sent over open systems without their content being read by any observers. Only those with whom you have securely shared a key will be able to decipher your messages.

FAQ

What does your product offer?
A system which uses well established cryptographic functions to encode text in such a way that allows the encoded messages to be sent the same way you send unencoded messages now.
Won't publishing how your system works make it easier to crack?
Just the opposite. Instead of relying on secrets which would make all messages vulnerable when they would inevitably be revealed, a published system using standards already vetted by cryptography experts will result in a system these same experts can analyze for any weaknesses.
